Choosing the Best

Light Beam Patterns

Beam patterns are essential for safe driving, as they provide clear visibility without blinding other drivers. The design of the beam pattern must be tailored to specific road conditions and, most importantly, to whether the vehicle is intended for left-hand or right-hand drive markets.

  • Low beam Pattern: Its sharp cut-off line prevents glare, illuminating the road ahead & sides.
  • High Beam Pattern: Projects farther and higher for open roads, minimising scatter for clear distant visibility.

High Beam Usage Tips

  • Use high beams wisely: Ideal for dark roads, they improve long-distance visibility.
  • Avoid glare: Switch to low beams when approaching vehicles are nearby.
  • Check alignment: Ensure high beams are properly aligned to prevent discomfort for others.

Selecting the Right Color Temperature

Warm yellow light is ideal for foggy, dusty, rainy and smoggy environments and well-suited for Indian weather conditions.

Cool white light ensures superior contrast and visibility all day long, ideal for clear sky conditions.

Blue-tinted light can cause glare and may not be road-legal in some countries, hence it is not recommended.
